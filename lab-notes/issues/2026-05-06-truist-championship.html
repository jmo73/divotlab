IF YOU PLAY
The Green Mile Is a Mental Test Before It's a Physical One
Quail Hollow's closing stretch — three holes, all demanding, all capable of wrecking a round — is what separates it from most courses. The professionals know it. The amateurs who play there know it. The problem isn't that golfers don't prepare for it physically; it's that they don't prepare for it mentally. By hole 16, your score is in your head. The calculation has already started — what you need, what you can't afford to lose. That calculation is exactly what gets people in trouble.
The practical fix isn't complicated. Before you play a course with a known closing stretch, decide what your strategy is on each hole before you tee off — not on the tee box with a score in mind, but in the car on the way there. If you're playing hole 17 defensively regardless of your score, you can commit to that decision when it matters. Golfers who play their worst closing holes almost always have the same problem: they changed their strategy based on score rather than executing a pre-committed plan. Make the decisions early. Then just play golf.
