IF YOU PLAY
Why the Short Approach Is the Shot You Should Be Practicing Most
Harbour Town's leaderboard this week was decided almost entirely by the 80-to-130-yard range. The course is short by Tour standards and tree-lined, which means the premium isn't on length — it's on precise yardage control into small greens with firm surroundings. Fitzpatrick, Kim Si Woo, and Morikawa all ranked in the top of the approach category. Scheffler's late charge was built on his short game, not his driver. The course is essentially a 72-hole test of the shot most amateurs spend the least time on at the range: the controlled half-swing iron from inside 150 yards.
Most amateurs hit full shots from 150 and punch shots from 60. The gap between those two — the 80-to-130-yard range where you need three-quarter swings with consistent contact — is where handicaps live. Find three yardages in that range that you genuinely trust and practice until they're automatic: a specific three-quarter 9-iron, a controlled pitching wedge, and a smooth gap wedge. Not full swings. The kind of controlled shots that land and stay. That range is where Harbour Town separates the field, and it's where your course separates scores too.
